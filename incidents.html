Frequently Asked Questions

How are incidents investigated?

Each report is carefully reviewed by our investigation team. We assess the credibility of the report and conduct site visits when necessary. Investigations typically take 2-4 weeks depending on complexity. All findings are documented and recommendations provided to facility management.

Can I report anonymously?

Yes! We strongly support anonymous reporting. You do not need to provide your name, email, or any identifying information. However, if you choose to provide contact information, we can keep you updated on the investigation progress.

What happens after I submit a report?

Once submitted, your report is logged into our system with a unique ID number (you'll receive this). Our team will assess the report, gather additional information if needed, and determine next steps. Critical welfare issues are prioritized for immediate investigation.

Is my information kept confidential?

Absolutely. We maintain strict confidentiality protocols. Your personal information is never shared with zoo management unless you explicitly authorize it. Reports can be investigated using facility records and third-party sources.

What if I'm concerned about retaliation?

We take whistleblower protection very seriously. Anonymous reporting protects you from identification. If you prefer non-anonymous reporting but are concerned, we can discuss protective measures before submission.

How can I track my report?

After submission, you'll receive a unique incident ID. If you provided an email address, you can use this to request updates. Check back on this page for general incident tracking information as well.
